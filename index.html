What is it

Symptoms Cause Each Other

Traditional psychometrics assumes that observed symptoms — sleeplessness, low mood, fatigue — are passive reflections of an underlying disorder. Network psychometrics inverts this assumption.

In the network approach, symptoms are causally interconnected. Insomnia causes fatigue; fatigue causes concentration difficulties; concentration difficulties worsen depressive rumination. The disorder is the network.

Developed from graph theory and statistical physics, this paradigm offers new tools for understanding individual differences, comorbidity, treatment pathways, and the topology of the psyche.

Core framework

Six Fundamental
Concepts

01
Nodes

Psychological variables — symptoms, traits, cognitive features — treated as active entities in a causal system rather than passive indicators of an unseen factor.

◉
02
Edges

Pairwise associations between nodes, typically estimated via partial correlations (EBIC-GLASSO) or Bayesian methods, representing genuine conditional dependencies.

─
03
Centrality

Measures such as strength, betweenness, and closeness identify the most influential variables — potential leverage points for intervention and treatment.

★
04
Communities

Clustering algorithms detect tightly connected subgroups within a network, revealing natural substructures that may correspond to symptom dimensions or facets.

⬡
05
Stability

Accuracy and stability of network estimates are evaluated through bootstrapping and case-dropping procedures, ensuring reproducible inference from finite samples.

⧖
06
Dynamics

Temporal networks capture how variables influence one another over time — transforming static snapshots into longitudinal causal portraits of psychological processes.
